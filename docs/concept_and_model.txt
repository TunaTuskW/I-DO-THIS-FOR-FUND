MACRO ANALYST AGENT - CONCEPT AND MODEL
=======================================

1. WHAT IT IS
This is a 24/7 autonomous quantitative agent that monitors global markets, processes data through deep learning classifiers, and generates institutional-grade macro reports. It runs completely automatically in the background using your computer's scheduler or inside isolated Docker microservices. The presentation layer uses an ultra-minimal "Brutalist Terminal" text-block layout to ensure a data-dense, cross-platform reading experience designed for instant mobile parsing.

2. THE MULTI-ASSET ENSEMBLE OS & CAPITAL ROTATION ENGINE (v5.1.0 Evolution)
Following the v5.1.0 upgrades, the agent operates as a containerized, event-driven Mixture of Experts (MoE) & Multi-Asset Ensemble OS. The architecture incorporates several critical structural and mathematical upgrades:

- Event-Driven Pub-Sub Architecture: Standardizes pipeline execution via an in-memory `EventBus` (`src/observability/event_bus.py`). Triggers sequential, decoupled callbacks across five lifecycle phases.
- Pydantic Validation Schemas: Enforces strict type-safety and structural data contracts utilizing Pydantic models (`src/schemas/models.py`) such as `MarketSnapshot` to eliminate data schema breaks.
- High-Impact Economic Calendaring: The calendar adapter (`ForexFactoryAdapter` under `src/adapters/forexfactory_adapter.py`) fetches weekly high-impact events for USD, EUR, and JPY currencies, parsing them into `EconomicCalendar` structures.
- Active-Resilient MoE Cognitive Layer: Integrates a dual-provider active-active LLM framework utilizing Google Gemini (`gemini-2.5-flash`) and Groq (`Llama 3`) models running in parallel.
- Parallel LLM Echo Chamber Detector: Detects if both parallel MoE experts (Policy and Psychology) run on the same LLM provider due to rate-limit failovers. Applies a 0.70x penalty to the News Conviction Score to mitigate correlated narrative bias.
- Dynamic Rolling Statistical Windows by Interval: Statistical z-score calculations in `feature_engine.py` adapt dynamically to the operational interval. It calculates bars dynamically to keep the lookback baseline at exactly **3 macro months (60 trading days equivalent)** across daily (`60` daily bars), hourly (e.g. `97` hourly bars for 4h), and weekly (`12` weekly bars) timeframes.
- Return-Based z-Scores: Statistical anomaly tracking is computed as **Return-Based z-Scores** ($z = (\Delta\% - \text{mean}) / \text{std}$ of percentage changes) to prevent price level drift.
- Treasury Yield & Spread z-Scores: Ingests 60-day yield return deltas (`delta_zscore` of US10Y) and 2s10s spread deltas (`spread_zscore`) in place of raw yield indices inside features engineered.
- GARCH Bayesian Updates & HMM Regularization: 
  * If the computed SPX GARCH regime is `ELEVATED` (proxy: vix_zscore > 1.0), the system executes a Bayesian contraction, automatically slashing HMM Risk-On probabilities by 50% and redistributing the penalized mass directly back to `NEUTRAL_TRANSITIONAL` to suppress bullish bias.
  * Enforces **Uniform HMM Start Probabilities** ($\text{startprob\_} = 1/N$) before scoring samples to prevent degenerate single-bar inference traps in low-sample environments.
- Dynamic Measurement Noise Covariance: To prevent sudden, single-bar HMM regime jumps that cause excessive trading turnover, the `RiskEngine` inflates the measurement noise covariance matrix to $R = \mathbf{I} \times 0.25$ (instead of $0.05$) when a sudden observation fear spike is detected ($z[1] > 0.6$ while prior $x[1] < 0.3$), requiring multi-bar confirmation.
- Dynamic Brier Self-Calibration Filter: Tracks historical forecasts against actual forward returns using a **5-bar delayed grading window**, dynamically adjusting the model's calibration penalty to reduce sizing during accuracy degradation.
- Ensemble Classifier Inference Routing: Ingests features into ensembles of three classifiers (MLP, Random Forest, Gradient Boosting) trained for S&P 500 (`spx`), Bitcoin (`btc`), Gold (`gld`), and Crude Oil (`wti`). Reports a Consensus Score based on prediction standard deviation.
- Auto-Inversion Calibration Module: Automatically flips a model's directional probability ($\text{prob} = 1.0 - \text{prob}$) if its rolling Brier Score rises above `0.60`, turning lagging performance into a contrarian hedge.
- Sizing Overrides & Safety Circuit Breakers: Evaluates indicators to trigger high-conviction overrides that bypass HMM regime penalties:
  * Capitulation Override (Contrarian Buy): Active if SPX return z-score is oversold between `-1.5` and `-3.0`, institutional volume support is high (`ihi > 0.0`), and MLP is bullish (`mlp_prob > 0.5`). Applies a **0.9x guarded contrarian multiplier**.
  * Momentum Ignition Override (Trend Buy): Active if SPX return z-score is strongly positive (`spx_ret_z > 1.0`), volume heat is positive (`ihi > 0.1`), and MLP probability is bullish ($0.4 < \text{mlp\_prob} \le 0.80$). Bypasses Brier penalties and applies an aggressive **1.25x momentum multiplier**.
  * Black Swan Circuit Breaker: Automatically liquidates all equity exposure (`SPX_Kelly = 0.0`) if the SPX return z-score falls below `-3.5` standard deviations.
  * Macro Trend Override: Blocks long positions (forces SPX Kelly to `0.0`) if SPX is in a technical downtrend (below 20 EMA).
  * Retail Noise Filter: Slashes S&P 500 Kelly exposure by 50% if the market is not risk-off but institutional heat is negative (`ihi < 0.0`).
- Capital Rotation Engine: Dynamically rotates capital (scales alternative allocations by 1.5x) to Gold (`gld`), Bitcoin (`btc`), and Oil (`wti`) if S&P 500 displays weakness ($\text{spx\_prob} < 0.40$). Normalizes total exposure to a hard limit of `1.2` (120% exposure) via the Global Portfolio Balancer.
- Regime-Specific Alternative Asset Filters: Forces crypto (`btc`) and energy (`wti`) Kelly exposure to 0.0 in panic regimes (`DEFLATION_FEAR` or `CRISIS_DISLOCATION`), while boosting Gold as a safe haven.
- Decoupled Consensus Engine Signal Mapping (`consensus_engine.py`): Voting conviction outputs are mapped directly into strict boundaries: `QUANT_DIVERGENCE_PANIC` (SHORT, 0.5x Kelly slash), `CONSENSUS_BEARISH` (SHORT), `CONSENSUS_BULLISH` (LONG, 1.2x sizing on expansion), and `MIXED_SIGNALS` (MIXED).
- Phase 2 Live Telemetry Engine: Writes a structured real-time operational payload to `/Users/mac/agent/data/live_telemetry.json` (tracking timestamp, dominant regime, SPX/Safe Haven Kelly fractions, override status, and volume heat) on pipeline completion.
- Yield Curve Proxy Fallbacks: Dynamically pulls proxy yields (`^TNX` for the 10Y and `^FVX` for the 5Y) directly from Yahoo Finance if FRED API keys are missing.
- Containerized OS: Standardizes execution in isolated containerized environments using Docker and Docker-Compose.

3. THE END-TO-END WORKFLOW
- Stage 1: `SystemStart` Event: The orchestrator Conductor (`src/fetch_market_data.py`) fires the start sequence, logging operational startup context. Ingests USD/EUR/JPY economic calendar events via ForexFactoryAdapter.
- Stage 2: `DataFetched` Event: Ingests global index prices, commodities, and yields. If the FRED key is missing, dynamically fetches yfinance bond proxies. Conductor invokes `LakeManager` to save raw Parquet dataframes.
- Stage 3: `FeaturesEngineered` Event: FeatureEngine processes stats, GARCH conditional volatility, volume heat, Gold-to-Silver ratio, and composite Credit Stress z-scores.
- Stage 4: `EnginesCompleted` Event: HMMEngine runs inference to map regime probabilities. RiskEngine computes updated Kalman states (validated as `KalmanState`) and Shannon Entropy indices. 
- Stage 5: `PipelineComplete` Event: Executes the dual LLM experts (Macro Policy and Market Psychology) in parallel using a ThreadPoolExecutor. The ConsensusEngine synthesizes their outputs, validates the merged CoT reasoning, checks quantitative divergence, and compiles the final `NewsSignal`.
- Sizing Multiplier & Lake Logging: Applies aggressive Kelly multipliers (up to 1.2x exposure during expansion, and 0.5x slashes during rate shocks or quantitative divergences). The final snapshot is validated as a Pydantic `MarketSnapshot` and written to a daily partitioned event log: `data/raw/YYYY/MM/DD/events.jsonl` via the interceptor.
- Presentation & Delivery: Report compilers (`src/build_report.py`, etc.) pull the snapshot directly from the daily partitioned event log and push brutalist briefings with MoE reasoning to Discord.

4. THE MATH & MODELS BEHIND THE SCENES
- Macro Condition Score (MCS): A composite number measuring overall market stress based on stock momentum, interest rates, energy prices, and cross-asset behavior (FeatureEngine).
- Hidden Markov Models (HMM) & Kalman Filters: These algorithms smooth out daily noise to figure out the "true" underlying regime (HMMEngine & RiskEngine).
- Deep Classifier (MLP): A trained neural network that double-checks the HMM. 
- Institutional Footprinting: Natively supports advanced institutional footprinting, including a 20-day continuous Institutional Heat Index (IHI).
- Multimodal MoE LLM Synthesis (Groq Llama 3 & Google Gemini Flash Engines): 
  Synthesizes live unstructured news feeds with hard quantitative indicators utilizing a dual-provider active-active structure:
  * Parameter Decoding: Macro and Psychology experts parse text and quant data to output strict JSON starting with exactly 3 sentences of step-by-step Chain-of-Thought (CoT) reasoning.
  * Resilient Failover & Failback: The Macro Policy Expert runs Groq as primary, failing over automatically to Gemini on error. The Market Psychology Expert runs Gemini as primary, failing over automatically to Groq on error.
  * API Call Resilience: Protects inference processes with a 10-attempt dynamic backoff loop that intercepts HTTP 429 and 503 errors and steps up sleep times by 10s per iteration to secure robust execution.
- Hyperparameter Tuning Meta-Agent (tune_hyperparameters.py):
  * Dynamic Parameter Tuning: A standalone meta-agent scheduled to run weekly. It feeds massive macroeconomic text blocks (such as FOMC meeting minutes or Beige Books) to the Gemini LLM.
  * Configuration Tuning: Translates central bank transcripts into concrete mathematical half-life decay thresholds and calibration scalars, dynamically overwriting active constants in `fetch_market_data.py` via the local `tuning_configs.json` configuration file.

5. ADVANCED MODEL GOVERNANCE & TACTICAL ASSUMPTIONS
- Aggressive Kelly Sizing (1.2x Kelly Sizing): Sizing is calculated via the Fractional Kelly Criterion, penalized by historical Brier Score calibration, exponential duration decay, and dominant regime state risk-aversion penalisers.
- Sizing Leverage Overdrive: If the consensus engine fires a `LONG` sentiment signal during a structural `RISK_ON_EXPANSION` regime, the system applies a **1.2x multiplier**, pushing target portfolio exposure bounds up to **120% Kelly size**.
- Capital Defense Slasher (0.5x Slasher): Slashes portfolio exposure by half (**0.5x multiplier**) during interest rate shocks, global economic contractions, quantitative narrative-reality divergence events, or whenever the active dominant HMM state maps to `risk_off` (such as STAGFLATION_STRESS, RATE_SHOCK, etc.).
- Transitional Regime Slasher (0.75x Slasher): Applies a dynamic **0.75x multiplier** (25% discount) to target Kelly sizing when the dominant HMM state maps to `transitional` to defend capital in directionless choppy ranges.
- 1.5 Std Dev Dynamic Boundaries: Continuously tracks volatility thresholds to automatically invalidate outdated narratives upon statistical sweeps.

6. ESCALATION SYSTEM
The agent respects your time and won't spam you with panicky alerts. It only flags urgency when the math demands it:
- ROUTINE: Normal market updates.
- ELEVATED: Moderate volatility spikes, sudden currency squeezes, or early-warning transition signals.
- CRITICAL: Extreme mathematical stress (e.g., VIX > 30, simultaneous stock and bond crashes). Discord will ping you heavily for these.

7. THE AUTOMATED SCHEDULE
The agent manages two distinct workflows natively via bash scripts (run_4h.sh, run_weekly.sh) executed by cron or docker scheduling:
- The 4-Hour Briefing: A quick pulse-check of the active market session.
- The Sunday Synthesis: A deep-dive research report summarizing the entire week and previewing the week ahead.
- *Note: Daily 72-hour roll schedules (`build_72h_roll.py` and `run_daily.sh`) have been completely removed to streamline the pipeline.*

8. OFFLINE MODEL TRAINING, BACKTESTING & VISUALIZATION
The machine learning models (HMM and Ensemble Classifiers) are not static. The agent includes a robust training pipeline (`train_models.py`), backtest engine (`quantitative_backtester.py`), and a visualizer script (`generate_visual_map.py`) to periodically adapt to evolving market regimes:

- The HMM Regime Training Pipeline:
  * Ingests 5 years of daily historical data across Equities (SPX), Volatility (GARCH volatility proxy), FX/Treasuries (DXY, USDCAD, 10Y yields, 10Y-2Y yield spread), Commodities (WTI Crude, Gold, Silver), and a Bitcoin volume volatility proxy.
  * Standardizes the aligned 10-dimensional feature vector using a StandardScaler.
  * Fits a Gaussian Hidden Markov Model (GaussianHMM) configured with N_HIDDEN_STATES = 6, a full covariance matrix structure, and 500 maximum EM iterations. Enforces uniform start probabilities (startprob_ = 1/N) to regularize posteriors.
  * Dynamically maps states by checking their emission means across daily SPX returns, yield shifts, and WTI moves:
    - RISK_ON_EXPANSION: Pos. SPX return & rising yields.
    - LIQUIDITY_DRIVEN_RALLY: Pos. SPX return & falling yields.
    - STAGFLATION_STRESS: Neg. SPX return & rising energy prices.
    - RATE_SHOCK: Neg. SPX return & expanding bond yields.
    - DEFLATION_FEAR: Neg. SPX return & falling energy prices.
    - CRISIS_DISLOCATION: Neg. SPX return (catch-all for severe negative drift).
    - NEUTRAL_TRANSITIONAL: Stable market conditions.

- The Supervised Multi-Asset Ensemble Pipeline:
  * Fits ensemble classifiers (MLP, Random Forest, and Gradient Boosting) for four separate assets (SPX, BTC, GLD, WTI) based on historical feature vectors.
  * Targets a 5-period forward cumulative return: Bull (y = 1, return > threshold), Bear (y = 0, return < -threshold), and Transitional (y = 2, otherwise).
  * Thresholds adapt dynamically to the interval (e.g. 1.5% for daily SPX, 3.0% for weekly SPX, scaled down for 4h/1h).

- The Historical Backtest Engine (quantitative_backtester.py):
  * Decodes historical features into regime labels, runs the Multi-Asset Kelly solver, and generates portfolio forward returns.
  * Compiles a detailed performance log (backtest_extended_results.md) detailing daily allocations (SPX Long/Short, BTC, GLD, WTI, Cash) and portfolio PnL, as well as a Deep Learning Probability Calibration table showing win rates and returns per probability bucket.

- The Stacked Portfolio Visualizer (generate_visual_map.py):
  * Replaces legacy Jupyter notebooks with a centralized Python script.
  * Parses backtest reports and plots high-resolution PNG charts (`reports/visualize_map.png`) showing:
    - S&P 500 price trajectory and regime states.
    - Ensemble probability calibration and threshold limits.
    - Stacked Kelly allocation areas representing S&P 500 (Long & Short), Bitcoin, Oil, Gold, and Cash rotation over time.

9. DATA PRIVACY & ZERO-SHARING SECURITY PROTOCOLS
To protect proprietary quantitative intelligence and restrict leakage of sensitive credentials, the system enforces a local-only encapsulation boundary:
- Strict Ignored Enclaves: The `.gitignore` config explicitly encapsulates all private credentials (`api_keys.json`), tuned configurations (`tuning_configs.json`), private asset snapshots (`market_snapshot.json`), forecast accuracies (`predictions_history.json`), model weights (`models/hmm_model.pkl`, `models/mlp_model.pkl`), run-time logs (`logs/`), and generated briefings (`reports/updates/`).
- Decoupled Example Schemas: Default templates (e.g., `api_keys.example.json`, `market_snapshot.example.json`) are committed to establish skeleton frameworks, which are duplicated locally to active formats. This ensures developers can execute backtests, Gemini news analysis, and offline hyperparameter tuning entirely offline and locally with zero risk of committing operational intelligence or API credentials to public repositories.

By anchoring the system to a rigid mathematical training and verification backbone, the pipeline provides institutional-quality insights with deterministic reliability, entirely free from LLM hallucinations.
